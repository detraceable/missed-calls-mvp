# 10 Design Outlines for Your Website

Use these as briefs for full redesigns or to mix-and-match sections. Each outline is self-contained: colors, typography, layout, and key patterns.

---

## 1. **Spatial Glass (Current)**
*Futuristic, Vision Pro–inspired, premium*

- **Vibe:** Deep space + frosted glass panels. Feels expensive and technically superior.
- **Background:** Dark base (#030014) with layered radial gradients (purple/indigo/blue). Subtle mesh drift animation.
- **Surfaces:** 1px semi-transparent borders, 40–80px backdrop blur, soft inset highlights. Three tiers: glass, glass-strong, glass-panel.
- **Accent:** Sky blue (#7dd3fc) primary; violet (#a78bfa) secondary for labels.
- **Type:** Clean sans (Inter) + display (Outfit). Tight tracking on headings, generous spacing.
- **Best for:** B2B SaaS, product-led, “we’re the future” positioning.

---

## 2. **Editorial Minimal**
*Magazine-style, confident, lots of whitespace*

- **Vibe:** High-end editorial. Big type, few elements, strong hierarchy.
- **Background:** Off-white (#fafafa) or pure white. Optional very light gray sections for contrast.
- **Surfaces:** No cards or heavy borders. Dividers are thin lines or extra spacing. Occasional subtle shadows on CTAs only.
- **Accent:** Single bold color (e.g. black, or one brand hue). No gradients.
- **Type:** Large serif or geometric sans for headlines (e.g. Fraunces, DM Serif Display); neutral sans for body. Huge hero numbers or stats.
- **Best for:** Premium positioning, thought leadership, “we don’t need to shout.”

---

## 3. **Neo-Brutalist**
*Bold, raw, high contrast*

- **Vibe:** Unapologetic. Sharp edges, thick borders, raw grids.
- **Background:** White or cream. Black used heavily for text and borders.
- **Surfaces:** 2–4px black (or dark) borders. Flat color blocks. No blur, no gradients. Optional single bright accent (yellow, red, green).
- **Type:** Bold, often monospace or slab. All caps for labels. Big, chunky headings.
- **Best for:** Dev tools, startups that want to stand out, “we’re different.”

---

## 4. **Warm SaaS**
*Friendly, trustworthy, approachable*

- **Vibe:** Soft and human. Rounded corners, warm neutrals, friendly copy.
- **Background:** Warm white or very light beige (#fdfcfa). Soft section backgrounds (light amber or sage).
- **Surfaces:** Rounded-2xl cards, soft shadows (warm gray). No harsh borders; use background tint to separate.
- **Accent:** Warm green, teal, or coral—nothing cold. Buttons pill-shaped.
- **Type:** Rounded sans (e.g. Nunito, Plus Jakarta Sans). Comfortable line height.
- **Best for:** SMB, local businesses, “we’re here to help.”

---

## 5. **Terminal / Dev**
*Code-first, dark, monospace*

- **Vibe:** Built for developers. Feels like a well-designed IDE or CLI.
- **Background:** True dark (#0d1117 or similar). Subtle noise or grid.
- **Surfaces:** Panels with thin borders (green or cyan). Syntax-highlight style for labels (e.g. purple for types, green for strings).
- **Accent:** Green or cyan. Monospace for code snippets and key metrics.
- **Type:** Monospace (JetBrains Mono, Fira Code) for code and numbers; clean sans for body.
- **Best for:** API products, dev tools, technical audience.

---

## 6. **Gradient Mesh**
*Vibrant, modern, marketing-forward*

- **Vibe:** Eye-catching. Gradients everywhere but controlled.
- **Background:** Gradient mesh (multiple overlapping blurs: brand colors). Can be dark or light.
- **Surfaces:** Glass or semi-transparent cards over the mesh. Gradient borders or gradient text for headlines.
- **Accent:** 2–3 brand colors in gradients (e.g. blue→purple, orange→pink).
- **Type:** Bold sans. Gradient text on hero headline. Rounded buttons with gradient fill.
- **Best for:** Launch pages, marketing sites, “vibrant and modern.”

---

## 7. **Corporate Clean**
*Enterprise, trustworthy, minimal*

- **Vibe:** “We’re the safe choice.” No flash, maximum clarity.
- **Background:** White. Light gray (#f5f5f5) for alternate sections.
- **Surfaces:** Simple cards with light border or shadow. Clear sections with consistent padding.
- **Accent:** One primary (often blue). Used sparingly: CTAs, links, key icons.
- **Type:** Neutral sans (Inter, Source Sans). Clear hierarchy: H1 → H2 → body. No decorative fonts.
- **Best for:** Enterprise, compliance-sensitive, “we’re reliable.”

---

## 8. **Retro / Y2K**
*Nostalgic, playful, bold*

- **Vibe:** Early 2000s web meets modern polish. Chrome, gradients, bubbly shapes.
- **Background:** Gradient (often purple/pink/blue) or texture. Optional “scan lines” or grain overlay.
- **Surfaces:** Rounded rectangles, glossy buttons (highlight at top). Optional 3D-ish shadows.
- **Accent:** Hot pink, electric blue, lime. Often used together.
- **Type:** Rounded or bubbly sans. Optional pixel or “digital” font for headings.
- **Best for:** Creative agencies, Gen Z audience, “we’re fun.”

---

## 9. **Documentation**
*Clarity-first, scannable, hierarchical*

- **Vibe:** Feels like a great docs site. Sidebar nav, clear headings, code blocks.
- **Background:** White or very light gray. Content area max-width for readability.
- **Surfaces:** Sidebar with subtle bg. Content cards with left border accent. Code blocks with distinct background.
- **Accent:** One color for links and “current” states. Breadcrumbs and “On this page” for long content.
- **Type:** Highly readable sans. Small caps or bold for nav labels. Monospace for code.
- **Best for:** Product docs, help centers, “find what you need fast.”

---

## 10. **Luxury / High-End**
*Refined, sparse, premium*

- **Vibe:** Expensive. Gold/black or black/white. Lots of space, few elements.
- **Background:** Black or very dark. Optional subtle texture (linen, paper).
- **Surfaces:** Thin gold or white borders. No heavy cards—floating elements with generous margin.
- **Accent:** Gold (#c9a227) or champagne. Used for key lines, icons, one CTA.
- **Type:** Serif for headlines (e.g. Playfair Display, Cormorant). Elegant sans for body. Large letter-spacing on labels.
- **Best for:** High-ticket services, luxury positioning, “we’re exclusive.”
